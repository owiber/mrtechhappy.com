I just got myself a bowl of ice cream (I try to have a bowl every night), and it served as a very painful reminder of one of the weird things about me. As most of you already now, I am almost immune to cold weather. I wear a short sleeve shirt pretty much all the time unless it's below 30°F, and am just fine standing outside in said attire. The caveat is that I cannot stand anything but the slightest wind, and I experience a very painful burning sensation (no, not THAT kind of burning sensation, you pervert) when I grasp something cold (i.e., an ice cream container) for more than a few seconds. I am well aware that moving air removes much more heat than still air and that most solids and liquids remove exponentially more than that, but everyone in my family can scoop ice cream just fine, yet gets cold fairly easily. The only possible explanation for this I've though of is that my outermost nerves take extreme temperatures without batting an eye (or whatever it is nerves bat), but those further in are abnormally sensitive. The worst part, however, is that my massive hands with their freakishly long and thin fingers are definitely not suited for gripping your average spoon and using it to scoop chunks out of what is, essentially, a solid mass, and the result is that I have to hold on to the frozen cylinder of doom for a much longer period of time than your average homo sapien. If ice cream wasn't so darn tasty and addictive, I definitely wouldn't go through the trouble.
