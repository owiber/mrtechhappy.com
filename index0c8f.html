A few days ago, I was reading one of the many subcomics at Bob and George which referred to creepy histories of Exmoor and Gevaudan. Being a fan of creepy histories, I did some searching to see what I could find. The only common link I could find between them was cryptozoology. In Exmoor, some unknown wildcats killed hundreds of sheep, but it was later discovered that some nearby owners of wildcats had simply released them when they became illegal. The beast of Gevaudan, on the other hand, is the most well documented case of an unknown monster type animal attacking the populace. Over a few years in the 1760s, it killed about 100 people (mostly women and children), almost all of which were documented by the French government. It was such as problem that King Louis XV became personally involved, and sent a number of wolf hunting experts to try to take care of it. They eventually killed a beast that may have been the source of the problems, but it's not exactly definite what happened to the corpse. Some records indicate it was lost before it got to the King, others say it was lost in a museum fire in the early 20th century. A few even indicate that it was found, and was a striped Hyena. In any case, it was such an important event in French history that almost everyone there knows about it (but for some reason few people outside the country do). Then, I made the very interesting discovery that "Le Pacte des Loups" (recently released in the US as "The Brotherhood of the Wolf") was based on the events. Now I have even greater interest in seeing that movie.
