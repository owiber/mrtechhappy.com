On today's show we're going to give you an inside look at how the comic is created. It all starts off some terribly amusing occurance in our daily lives which we decide to turn into a comic. The next step generally involves us discovering that the incident in question does not lend itself well to comic form. Since it is 2:30 am by this time, we have to come up with an idea - and fast. We gather all our marketing research and decide what our target audience would find most amusing, in this case: the GameCube. We fire off ideas rapid-fire style, and whoever comes up with a bad idea receives a severe electric shock. Once we have an idea, we start making the comic (we're far too good for scripts and storyboards). I feed Oliver the text and scenarios, whipping him every so often for incentive. The comic is soon finished and uploaded onto the server. You may be surprised to find that, despite my iron rule, the comic itself is quite dynamic, and could have one of many endings. Alternate endings for today's comic include:
1. MARK: "Chad, you didn't make Brook's bubble airtight! That ruins the illusion!"
2. OLIVER: "Can't… breathe… *gasp*" (Oliver's ball wobbles back and forth a couple times and comes to a rest as he loses conciousness)
MARK: "Hey, my PokeBall stopped wobbling! That means Oliver accepts me as his new master!"
3. CHAD: "My monkey isn't getting anywhere near 200mph. That's awfully disappointing."
4. CHAD: (rolling Brook off a pier) "Now remember, aim for the targets worth a lot of points!"
BROOK: "What targets? All I see are boats!"

As you can see, any one of these would result in hilarity, but only the free range, hand-picked endings can reach that comic "zen." The lack of hormone supplements just makes it taste all the more sweet.
